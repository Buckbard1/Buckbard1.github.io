Canary Works 2.0

Futurism design and strategies.

The phrase "canary in the coal mine" dates back to industry in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Coal miners would bring canaries down in the mines because they were more sentitive to deadly gases than the miners. It has since transcended its literal origin to become a metaphor used in our modern contexts. Now it signifies someone who might be a harbinger of the future.

Canary Works is a platform for Buck Bard and other futurists to share their works in creating "canaries." All of us can be canaries, steering and shaping the future.

WE DON'T BELIEVE IN FATE

How many times have you heard that the future is already written, we're just playing it out? Or that there is a divine plan that we have to just accept? Nonsense. We agree with James Cameron when he said, "The future is not set. There is no fate but what we make."

There is crystal ball or Magic 8-Ball that can tell us our future. That's because our future is constantly unfolding ahead of us, influenced by every decision we make. That means that fate is what we make it. Of course, if you just blindly float through life without intention it can seem like we're just players in someone else's story. And all too often we do just that!"

So in July 2024 we're relaunching Canary Works from an innovative L&D consultancy to a Futurist Practice. Our canaries have become harbingers of the future. Together we will explore various methods to shape our future for the better. We will dream and prototype and share ideas.

We will design our futures together, not because we should but because we must."
